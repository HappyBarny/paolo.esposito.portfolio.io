Introduction

In this project I have worked with my mate Rossana D'onofrio and I'm truly excited to introduce you to Woo, a dating app designed to revolutionize online dating.

The online dating market is more active than ever, with Tinder hosting 75 million active users monthly and competitors like Bumble, Hinge, and Cupid offering unique approaches. Each app focuses on specific aspects: Tinder emphasizes appearances, Hinge highlights profile depth, and Cupid matches based on compatibility ratings. However, through our research—using these apps ourselves and gathering feedback via questionnaires—we identified recurring issues:


  • Trust Issues: Users often doubt if the person they’re talking to matches their profile.

  • Passive Usage: Many treat dating apps like TikTok, scrolling without engagement.

  • Short-lived Conversations: Chats often fizzle out quickly, leading to missed opportunities for real connections.

  • Difficulty Transitioning to Real-life Meetings: Users struggle to turn matches into meaningful interactions.

These challenges resonate with findings like Tinder’s "Green Flag Research" and Hinge’s annual insights, which show an increase in passive users and fake profiles. For example, 44% of users don’t actively respond to messages, and most likes don’t lead to conversations. This disengagement results in fewer meaningful matches.

Why Woo?

Woo aims to rebuild trust and foster genuine connections. It’s not just about finding a date but about connecting people with shared interests and clear intentions.
Whether it’s finding a running partner or someone to join a cooking class, Woo adapts to user needs.

We’ve created user personas and designed the app to encourage interaction and real-life meetups. By focusing on active participation and shared goals, Woo seeks to create a better dating experience. Woo isn’t just about finding someone—it’s about building trust and meaningful connections.

The idea

Woo is built around the idea of reframing the dating app experience to meet diverse user needs. Whether you’re looking for love or simply want to find a gym buddy or a friend for a cooking class, Woo personalizes the experience around what matters most to you.

  1. Limited Daily Interactions:
    • Users can swipe on a maximum of 10 profiles daily, with up to 3 matches per day.
    • This limitation encourages meaningful connections by reducing the overwhelm of endless swiping.

  2. Focused Conversations
    • A maximum of 6 open chats ensures users can dedicate attention to discovering and building deeper connections with their current matches.

  3. Progressive Profile Unlocking:
    • Over time, users can unlock additional parts of a match’s profile (like in a video game), encouraging conversations to continue over multiple days.

  4. Encouraging Real-life Meetings:
    • Woo promotes real-world interactions by offering features like buying tickets for shared activities such as cooking classes or live-streamed concerts.

  5. Enhanced Profile Verification:
    • User profiles are verified through live selfies, phone number checks, and email verification to foster a safer, more trustworthy community.

Developing the prototype

The first thing we did was clarify how the chat should work, based on our research. We created a series of sketches and drawings on paper to explore how people should interact within the app.

We decided to focus on one type of user: the classic dating app match where you connect with someone you like or want to date. We made decisions about how profiles would look, how users would interact with them, how sections of the profile would unlock over time, and how to notify users that more content was available on their match's profile.



We developed a simple user flow centered on two main goals of the app: We developed a simple user flow centered on two main goals of the app:

  • Meet People
  • Find Events to Attend Together

As I sad before, the app includes features to discover events like cooking classes or live-streamed concerts, designed to encourage real-life interactions.
By limiting daily swipes (maximum 10), matches (maximum 3), and open chats (maximum 6), Woo ensures users focus on meaningful conversations and connections. Over time, users can unlock new parts of their matches' profiles, encouraging longer conversations. Profiles are verified through live selfies, phone number checks, and email verification to create a safe and trustworthy environment.

Remember: for this project we had just a week.
It quickly became clear that we couldn’t develop a fully functional prototype for our entire concept, so we chose to focus on a specific portion of it.
We created a user flow illustrating the "Journey of a person meeting their next date on our app." This journey begins with the login process, demonstrates how the chat functions, and shows where and how information about the date is displayed.
